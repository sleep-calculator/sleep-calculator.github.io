    The Science of Better Sleep

    The human body doesn't just "sleep" for several hours; it moves through distinct 90-minute sleep cycles. Each cycle consists of light sleep, deep sleep, and REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep.

    Why Sleep Cycles Matter

    If you wake up in the middle of a deep sleep stage, you'll likely experience "sleep inertia"—that feeling of being groggy, disoriented, and tired despite having slept for a long time. Our Sleep Calculator helps you time your alarm so you wake up at the end of a cycle, making the transition to wakefulness smooth and natural.

    Tips for a Perfect Night's Rest

    • Consistency is Key: Try to go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends.
    • Mind the Light: Reduce blue light exposure from screens at least an hour before bed.
    • Cool Environment: Your bedroom should be slightly cool (around 65°F or 18°C) for optimal sleep.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is the 15-minute fall-asleep time accurate?

    While 15 minutes is the average time it takes for a healthy adult to drift off, it can vary. If you know you fall asleep faster or slower, you can adjust your bedtime accordingly.

    How many hours of sleep should I get?

    Most adults need 7.5 to 9 hours of sleep per night to function at their best. This correlates to roughly 5 or 6 complete sleep cycles.
